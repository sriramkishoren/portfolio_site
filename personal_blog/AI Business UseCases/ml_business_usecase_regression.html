Business Applications of Regression

Forecasting numbers, understanding relationships, and defining the "Problem Space" in industry.

Core Concept Predicting a Response Variable ($Y$)

The Problem Space

Regression is not just about a formula; it is about defining a mathematical space of variables. It involves identifying inputs (customer attributes, history, environment) to forecast a specific meaningful number.

  • [cite_start]Input: Known variables (X) permitted for use [cite: 13-14].
  • [cite_start]Output: A response variable or target number ($Y$)[cite: 28].

Example: The Regression Equation

$y = a + bx$

[cite_start]Where $y$ might be Sales and $x$ might be Marketing Spend [cite: 39]

Crucial Distinction: Regression vs. Classification

Not all prediction problems are Regression. Regression is strictly for continuous outcomes (numbers). If the outcome is categorical (Yes/No), it is a different type of problem.

Not Regression

Predicting Loan Default

Outcome: "Yes" or "No"

Why: Outcome is not continuous.

Is Regression

Predicting Credit Limit

Outcome: $5,000, $10,500, etc.

Why: Outcome is a continuous number.
