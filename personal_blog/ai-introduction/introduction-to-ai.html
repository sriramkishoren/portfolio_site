The Core Framework: Sense, Comprehend, Act

Despite varying definitions, most AI systems follow a fundamental operational loop.

1. Sense

Perceiving the world via data inputs.

  • Camera/Image capture
  • Sensors (IoT, Temperature)
  • Database scraping
  • Audio recording

2. Comprehend

Processing data to extract meaning.

  • Pattern recognition
  • Predictive analytics
  • Language understanding
  • Anomaly detection

3. Act

Automating action or decision support.

  • Opening automatic doors
  • Recommending stock trades
  • Generating medical reports
  • Ordering inventory

AI Summers, Winters & Hype Cycles

AI development is cyclical. Understanding the "Gartner Hype Cycle" helps manage expectations and avoid disillusionment.

  1. Innovation Trigger: A breakthrough sparks interest.
  2. Peak of Inflated Expectations: Early publicity creates unrealistic hype.
  3. Trough of Disillusionment: Interest wanes as experiments fail to deliver.
  4. Slope of Enlightenment: Practical applications and best practices emerge.
  5. Plateau of Productivity: Mainstream adoption and proven value.

Conceptualizing AI Types

We distinguish between what is theoretically possible (General) and what exists today (Narrow).

General AI

A machine with human-like consciousness and self-awareness. It can perform any intellectual task a human can.

Narrow AI

Current Reality

‚ñº Two Main Approaches ‚ñº

Probabilistic AI

(Machine Learning)

  • Uses statistics & probability.
  • Pros: Handles messy data.
  • Cons: Results vary (Variance).

Deterministic AI

(Symbolic AI)

  • Uses rigid rules (If-Then).
  • Pros: Accurate & Provable.
  • Cons: Brittle; fails in new scenarios.

Machine Learning Types

"A computer program is said to learn if its performance (P) at tasks (T) improves with experience (E)." ‚Äî Tom Mitchell

Supervised Learning

Task: Classification / Regression

Human provides labeled data (X's and O's). Algorithm learns to map inputs to outputs.

Example: Spam filters, Medical diagnosis.

Unsupervised Learning

Task: Clustering / Association

Algorithm finds patterns in unlabeled data. Human interprets the clusters.

Example: Customer segmentation, Market basket analysis.

Reinforcement Learning

Task: Learn Decision Policy

Agent learns through trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties.

Example: AlphaGo, Insurance pricing, Robotics.
