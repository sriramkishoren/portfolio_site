Query Expressiveness

The efficiency of how you request data directly impacts memory and storage performance. Optimizing what you ask for (Calculus) vs. how you ask for it (Algebra) can prevent bottlenecks.

Relational Calculus Approach

Declarative (asking for "what"). Often more efficient as the optimizer determines the best execution path, reducing memory load.

Relational Algebra Approach

Procedural (asking "how"). Risk of manual inefficiencies if specific operations aren't optimized, leading to higher I/O.

Green AI Strategies

Location Matters

Train in regions with renewable energy or lower cooling costs.

Efficient Cooling

Cooling can be 30-40% of data center energy. Liquid cooling improves PUE.

Transfer Learning

Fine-tune existing models instead of training from scratch to save vast energy.

Model Efficiency

"It shocks me how little attention is applied to making models efficient."

Standard Model
  • Billions of Parameters
  • High Latency
  • High Cost
Optimized Model
  • Compressed Size (50-90% less)
  • Fast Inference
  • Low Cost

Pruning

Removing unnecessary weights or neurons. Can reduce model size by 50-90% with minimal accuracy loss.

Quantization

Reducing numerical precision (e.g., FP32 to INT8). 2-4x speedup and 4x memory reduction.

Distillation

Training a small "student" model to mimic a large "teacher". Student is 3-10x smaller.

Critical Challenges

Hardware Cost

GPUs are expensive ($30+/hr cloud). Costs accumulate rapidly during training.

Memory Wall

Model sizes grow faster than VRAM. Forces complex strategies like offloading.

Data Pipeline

Slow storage/preprocessing starves GPUs. The fastest chip waits for data.

Talent Scarcity

Optimal tools (CUDA) limit the talent pool. High labor costs vs. cheap hardware.

8 Key Performance Strategies

1 Right-Size Hardware

Match resources to workload. Don't use an H100 for small tabular data.

2 Optimize Memory

Use mixed precision (FP16) and gradient checkpointing to fit larger batches.

3 Model Compression

Invest in pruning and quantization (INT8) for production models.

4 Efficient Pipelines

Ensure data loading (SSD + Workers) doesn't starve the GPU.

5 Leverage Parallelism

Use Data, Model, or Pipeline parallelism to scale beyond single-machine limits.

6 Manage Energy

Track carbon footprint. Train in renewable regions. Optimize for efficiency.

7 Optimize Inference

Use TensorRT, dynamic batching, and caching to meet low-latency SLAs.

8 Holistic Monitoring

Establish performance budgets and continuously profile to find bottlenecks.
