Introduction to AI Resources

Computational Tradeoffs and Optimization Strategies for AI Projects at Scale

Delivering a successful AI project isn't just about code—it's about balancing memory, compute, query expressiveness, and performance. Learn which levers to adjust to avoid cost inefficiencies and deliver pragmatic solutions.

The 4 Pillars of AI Resources

The fundamental computational resources you must manage.

Memory

How much computational memory is taken up by your data and model.

  • Determines dataset size limits
  • Impacts real-time capability
  • Critical for large models

Compute Power

The processing capability required to execute algorithms and operations.

  • Hardware/Machinery tradeoffs
  • Computational tricks can optimize this
  • Major cost driver in cloud

Query Expressiveness

Not just asking for info, but how you ask and the database type used.

  • Database type selection
  • Complexity of retrieval
  • Impacts ability to deliver solutions

Performance

The ultimate output quality, speed, and reliability of the solution.

  • Result of balancing other pillars
  • Precision vs. Recall balance
  • Speed vs. Accuracy

Industry Concepts & Infrastructure

GPU vs. CPU

CPUs are for general logic. GPUs (Parallel processing) are critical for training Neural Networks but cost more.

Training vs. Inference

Training is resource-heavy (building the brain). Inference is using it. Optimize them separately.

Vertical vs. Horizontal

Vertical Scaling: Bigger machine (more RAM). Horizontal: More machines. AI often needs distributed horizontal scaling.

Edge vs. Cloud

Processing on device (Edge) saves bandwidth/latency but has low compute power. Cloud has power but high latency.

Batch vs. Real-time

Real-time requires low latency and high availability. Batch processing is cheaper and can happen off-peak.

Memory Types

VRAM: Video RAM on GPUs is the bottleneck for large model training. RAM: System memory for data preprocessing.

Key Takeaways for Success

1

Balance Precision & Recall: Decide early if you need high volume (recall) or high quality labels (precision).

2

Be Skeptical of "Standard" Advice: Online courses and cloud providers may push resource-heavy solutions that inflate costs.

3

Holistic Understanding: Success isn't just code; it's hardware, data strategy, and business alignment working together.

4

Pull the Right Levers: You can adjust requirements, data quality, algorithms, or hardware to fix project issues.
