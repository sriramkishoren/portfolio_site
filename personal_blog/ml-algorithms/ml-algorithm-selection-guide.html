10 Key Considerations

Technical Business Performance

Bias vs. Variance

  • Bias: Does it hit the target or miss the mark?
  • Variance: How "spread out" are the answers? Is the shot group tight?

Complexity

Often inversely related to interpretability. More complex models can find deeper patterns but become "black boxes."

Trade-off: Higher complexity usually means harder to explain.

Interpretability

Can you explain WHY the decision was made?

Critical for:

Loan approvals, legal compliance, medical diagnosis.

Accuracy

The precision of the returned answer. Often the primary goal, but not the only one.

Dilemma: A highly accurate model that you can't explain might be useless in regulated sectors.

Data Availability

Small Data Simpler Algos
Huge Data Deep Learning

Time & Cost

  • Training time (hours vs days)
  • Compute/Cloud costs

Lighter algorithms = faster response & lower cost.

Scalability

Toy Project Enterprise

Can it handle millions of pages/records in a production environment? Some algorithms choke on production-scale data.

Performance

Evaluate based on your specific application goals.

Precision
Recall
Specificity

Generalization vs. Specialization

Specialized Excellent at one specific task/dataset.
Generalizable Handles novel/unseen data effectively.

Robust vs. Flexible

  • Robust: Resilient to noise and outliers (stable).
  • Flexible: Adapts to complex, non-linear patterns.
Trade-off: High flexibility can lead to overfitting (less robust).

The Great Trade-offs

Balancing competing priorities in algorithm selection

Model Sophistication
Fast training, Cheap compute (e.g. Naive Bayes) Long training, Expensive compute (e.g. Random Forest/NN)

Decision Checklist

Does the problem require a specialized solution or general adaptability?

Do I need to explain the "Why" to a user or regulator?

Is the data volume sufficient for complex algorithms (Deep Learning)?

Can we afford the compute time/cost for training and inference?

Will this scale to production levels (millions of records)?
