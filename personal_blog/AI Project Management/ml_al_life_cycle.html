End-to-End Life Cycle

AI Use Case Categories

Before starting the lifecycle, understand which of the three common buckets your problem falls into. AI is either making decisions, automating processes, or personalizing interactions.

Decision Support

Enhancing human decision making with data-driven insights (e.g., Strategic forecasting, Risk assessment).

Process Automation

Automating physical or office processes (e.g., Claims processing automation, Robotic Process Automation).

Personalized Interaction

Tailoring experiences to individuals (e.g., Recommender systems, Chatbots, Search applications).

Phase Deep Dives

Requirements Phase

1. Problem Definition / Business Understanding

Key Activities

  • Align requirements with business strategy
  • Define decision support vs automation vs interaction
  • Identify gaps or inefficiencies in business

Critical Question

"Is this a problem AI can actually solve, and does it align with our strategic vision?"

2. Data Collection

  • • Operationalize collection & storage
  • • Generalizability Check: Does external data (e.g., Chicago crime) apply to our context (Baltimore)?
  • • Address privacy & security permissions

3. Data Prep & Engineering

  • • ETL: Extract, Transform, Load
  • • Harmonization: Integrating disparate data sources
  • • Feature Extraction: Creating meaning from raw inputs
  • • Labeling: Ensuring consistent annotation

4. Model Building

  • • Algorithm Selection
  • • Parameter Tuning
  • • The Black Box: Writing the ML code (Small part of total effort)

5. Model Evaluation

  • • Verification & Validation
  • • Assessing Bias
  • • Interpreting Results
Deployment Phase

6. Deployment & 7. Monitoring

Deployment Activities

  • Process Engineering: Changing workflows to accommodate AI
  • Change Management: Ensuring staff buy-in
  • Output Visualization: Making results usable

Monitoring & Maintenance

  • Drift Detection: Ensuring model stays accurate
  • Retraining Strategy: Periodic updates
  • Resource Management: Controlling costs/compute

Foundation: Responsible AI

Underlie all activities with Responsible AI from a human baseline perspective, not an overly fearful risk-based approach. Ensure AI supports business value, not the other way around.
