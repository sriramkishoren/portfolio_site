Size-Consistency-Quality Tradeoffs in AI Data

Understanding the Critical Balance for Successful AI Systems

Based on lectures by Dr. Ian McCullough, Johns Hopkins University

"You don't get good autonomous vehicles by training them on lots and lots of bad drivers. What you need to do is train them on a few good drivers."

CRITICAL: "If you do not get consistency right up front, you can't fix precision later."

The Three Critical Dimensions

Every AI project must balance these three competing factors. The optimal balance point depends on your specific use case.

Size (Volume)

  • Amount of training data available
  • Enables better generalization
  • Directly impacts Recall
OPTIMALBALANCE

Consistency (Uniformity)

  • Uniformity of annotations
  • Measured by Inter-Annotator Agreement
  • Directly impacts Precision

Quality (Accuracy)

  • Accuracy reflecting ground truth
  • Correctness and completeness
  • Foundation of reliability

The Core Metrics: Precision vs. Recall

Using the Autonomous Vehicle Pedestrian Detection Example

PRECISION

TP / (TP + FP)

"When I say it's a pedestrian, is it?"

Driven by Consistency. Ensures the model isn't hallucinating or confused.

Low Precision Scenario

The car stops violently because it thinks a mailbox is a child.

RESULT: Poor User Experience / Trust

RECALL

TP / (TP + FN)

"Do I see ALL the pedestrians?"

Driven by Data Size. Ensures the model handles variety and doesn't miss things.

Low Recall Scenario

The car fails to see a child crossing the street and does not stop.

RESULT: Safety Failure / Fatality

The Bottom Line: You cannot fix Precision retroactively. If trained on inconsistent data, the confusion is baked in.

Advantages of Large Data

Better Generalization

Model handles new, unseen scenarios (e.g., new cities for self-driving cars).

Wider Pattern Variety

Captures edge cases, rare scenarios, and nuanced distinctions.

Mitigates Overfitting

Prevents memorization; forces learning of general rules.

Disadvantages of Large Data

Resource Intensive

High GPU/TPU costs, electricity usage, and long training cycles.

Storage & Handling

Requires distributed systems, complex governance, and data movement.

Diminishing Returns

Eventually, more data stops improving performance but costs continue to rise.

10 Common Quality Issues

Incomplete

Missing fields or null values prevents feature learning.

Inconsistent

"USA" vs "United States". Creates category confusion.

Noisy

Sensor errors or environmental measurement spikes.

Imbalanced

99% Negative, 1% Positive. Bias towards majority.

Redundant

Duplicate entries overweight specific examples.

Human Bias

Annotator prejudices baked into the labels.

Label Errors

"Fat-fingering" or wrong classification.

Apportionment

Rare classes naturally underrepresented in real world.

Sparsity

Insufficient data in specific feature regions.

Data Drift

World changes over time; data no longer reflects reality.

5 Management Strategies

1

Active Learning

Iteratively select the most informative data points for labeling.

Result: Get to market quickly with MVP. Efficient budget use.
2

Data Augmentation

Create synthetic data or resample existing data (rotations, flips, Generative AI).

Result: Boost size while maintaining high consistency standards.
3

Transfer Learning

Fine-tune large foundation models (BERT, ResNet) instead of training from scratch.

Result: Drastically reduces data volume requirements.
4

Quality Control

Multiple annotators, Inter-Annotator Agreement (IAA) scores, and overlap management.

Result: Validated reliability. Start with high overlap, reduce as trust builds.
5

Combining Data Sources

Mix small high-quality "Gold" sets with larger, noisier sets to balance trade-offs.

Result: Risk diversification and comprehensive coverage.

Leadership Responsibility

Data quality issues are not just technical problems to be outsourced. They are business decisions involving trade-offs between cost, speed, and risk.

Engage productively in data decisions
Allocate resources for annotation & quality
Understand the "Can't fix precision later" rule

Key Questions for Leaders

  • What is our current Inter-Annotator Agreement score?
  • Are we scaling volume before proving consistency?
  • How are we handling rare classes in our ontology?
  • Is our overlap strategy adequate for this complexity?
