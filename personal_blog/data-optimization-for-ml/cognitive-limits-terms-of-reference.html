Human factors affecting data annotation quality in machine learning. Based on the insights of Dr. Ian McCullough (Johns Hopkins University).

"It's really important to think not just about the annotators, but what are the terms of reference, and what is the detail you're asking people to annotate."

The 5 Core Cognitive Limitations

Attention & Focus

Annotation requires sustained high-level attention. Performance degrades rapidly when attention is pulled.

Disaster Factors: Phone calls, hallway conversations, proximity to kitchens/cafeterias.

Memory Constraints

Large label sets overwhelm working memory (Miller's Law: 7±2 items).

Solution: Iterative Passing Label one criterion first, then review again for the next. Slower, but higher quality.

Cognitive Load

Complex text (big words, run-on sentences) + complex tasks = Fatigue & Overwhelm.

Impact: Annotators slow down and stop labeling carefully when load exceeds capacity.

Subjectivity & Bias

Definitions of "offensive" or "harmful" vary by person. Gray areas are inevitable.

Critical Question: Have you established clear standards? Do you know who is making the subjective calls?

Fatigue & Boredom

"Labeling data is not a fun task." It is monotonous and prone to quality degradation over time.

Mitigation: Frequent breaks, split assignments (annotate part-time), and recognizing fatigue early.

Terms of Reference

The "Terms of Reference" defines exactly what details you are asking annotators to label. If this exceeds their ability to observe or process, data quality collapses.

The "Counter-IED" Example

In Iraq, soldiers were asked to report on roadside bombs (IEDs) after an ambush.

The Data Request Identify 125+ technical features + environmental factors (garbage, animal remains, etc.).
The Human Context High stress. Friend potentially injured. Worried about secondary attacks/overwatch.

"Are you asking people to label data that they have no way of doing accurately?"

Social Desirability Bias

Why do annotators provide wrong answers instead of leaving fields blank?

  • They don't want to seem "incomplete" or not thorough in their job.
  • They don't want people to think they are "stupid" for not knowing.

The Result

Inaccurate mislabels that poison the AI training data.

Case Study: The Realtor Chatbot

Crowdsourcing classification of chat transcripts (Buyer, Renter, Seller, Other)

Relevance: This study proves that poor model performance is often a human problem, not a code problem. Initial low accuracy wasn't due to bad algorithms, but because independent annotators had undefined Terms of Reference. They were guessing based on subjective bias.

Management Best Practices

Clear Instructions

Crucial "must-have". Instructions must be tested to ensure they actually drive consistency.

Regular Breaks

Combat monotony. Schedule frequent breaks or rotation shifts to prevent quality degradation.

Measure Agreement

Measure Inter-Annotator Agreement (IAA) with overlap. Use this metric to validate interventions.

Use Automation

Use tools (dropdowns, highlighting) to handle complexity without exceeding human cognitive limits.

Training & Calibration

Don't assume reading = competence. Verify annotators are up to standard before they start.

Supplementary Context

Cognitive Load Types

  • Intrinsic Load Inherent difficulty of the task. Can only be managed by breaking tasks down.
  • Extraneous Load Caused by bad design or confusing tools. Reduce this to improve quality!
  • Germane Load Effort used for learning. Preserve capacity for this.
