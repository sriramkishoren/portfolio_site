DATA LABELING CONSIDERATIONS

Quality, Consistency, Privacy, and Management Practices for AI Training Data

Based on the lecture by Dr. Ian McCullough, Johns Hopkins University

"This is possibly one of the most important topics for a manager to understand... This is something that the business has to engage in with the technical team to make sure that you're building an AI solution that is performative."

The 5 Pillars of Labeling Strategy

Consistency & Quality

  • Foundation of high-performing AI systems.
  • Requires clear, management-approved guidelines.
  • Objective criteria to drive annotator agreement.

Scalability

  • Balance between volume vs. monitoring overlap.
  • Strategy: High overlap initially to identify issues, reduce as consistency stabilizes.

Subjectivity

  • Diverse opinions are good, but must converge on a standard.
  • Example: Defining "extremist" vs "negative" sentiment requires business decisions, not just labeler opinion.

Data Privacy

  • Protection of PII, Medical, Financial, and HR records.
  • Requires anonymization, masking, and strict access controls before labeling starts.

Scheduling & Cost

  • Labeling is not free. Budget for labor + tech resources.
  • Plan for contingencies. Cutting quality control to save money = Project Failure.

Types of Errors

Type 1: Recognition Error

Failing to identify an item that should be labeled.

Outcome: Incomplete but usable data

Type 2: Classification Error (SEVERE)

Recognizing an item but applying the wrong label.

Outcome: Untrustworthy data (Discard)

"You can't trust the data... much worse than recognition errors."

6 Best Practices for Success

1

Clear Guidelines

Must be written, detailed, and formally approved by management to align with company values.

2

Continuous QC

Never sacrifice quality control. Sample work regularly with a trusted annotation manager.

3

Training & Calibration

Train all annotators. Run calibration sessions to ensure everyone applies labels identically.

4

Iterative Labeling

Do multiple passes to prevent detail errors.

Pass 1: Nouns → Pass 2: People → Pass 3: Actions
5

Use Technology

Use AI to mitigate human bias and attention fatigue where practical.

6

Overlap Strategy

Start with high overlap to catch issues, reduce overlap as consistency is proven.

Final Takeaways

Quality of data > Quantity of data.
Bias usually comes from labeling, not the algorithm.
Measure K-Alpha & Vitality constantly. Target > 0.7.
Classification errors are fatal to model trust.
Management MUST set the subjectivity standards.
Always plan for "Recognition Errors" vs "Classification Errors".
